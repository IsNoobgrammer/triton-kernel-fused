The mechanism

One triangle, then a mirror

In each Newton-Schulz iteration A = X·Xᵀ and A·A are symmetric. So the kernel computes only the upper-triangle output tiles, transpose-copies each to its mirror, and skips the lower triangle entirely — about half the GEMM FLOPs. The non-symmetric B·X stays on cuBLAS. We add a batch dimension so it serves Muon's many same-shape parameters in one launch, and fuse the polynomial b·A + c·A² into the kernel epilogue.

computed mirrored (free) 
# Newton-Schulz iteration — 2 of 3 GEMMs are symmetric
A = symmul(X)              # X·Xᵀ   — triangle + mirror  (~½ FLOPs)
B = symmul_axpy(A, b, c)   # b·A + c·A²   — fused epilogue (~½ FLOPs)
X = baddbmm(X, B, X, β=a)  # a·X + B·X — cuBLAS (not symmetric)

Benchmark · Newton-Schulz step

Five implementations, one matrix, swept to 8192²

Same Polar-Express Newton-Schulz (5 steps) in every bar except flash-muon (its own Jordan recipe). eager = naive PyTorch, compiled = torch.compile, ours-fused = our cuBLAS+baddbmm NS, flash = nil0x9/flash-muon verbatim, ours-amalg = the symmetric-matmul NS.

ours-amalg leads at every size ≥2048. flash loses at 1024 (kernel launch > tiny GEMM) but its symmetric trick pulls ahead of dense cuBLAS by 4096 — and ours-amalg stays ahead of flash by batching + the fused epilogue.

Parity · the part that matters

Faster, bit-for-bit as accurate

The symmetric kernel changes only the order of floating-point adds (triangle+mirror vs full bmm), not the math. Against an fp32 reference of the same recipe, ours-amalg matches ours-fused exactly (max|Δ| 8.5e-5–2.5e-4) and lands 30–200× under the Newton-Schulz tolerance. Singular values are identical to the cuBLAS path. There is no precision tradeoff — it replaces FusedMuon outright on Blackwell.

The ceiling, honestly

Why ~1.5× and not more

Only 2 of the 3 Newton-Schulz GEMMs are symmetric; B·X is a full cuBLAS tensor-core GEMM at 371 TFLOP/s — near speed-of-light, 46% of the step (torch.profiler), and irreducible by symmetry. fp8 was tested and rejected: _scaled_mm ran slower than fp16 here and fp8 shattered orthogonalization (SV 0.001→2.2). So ~1.5× is the algorithmic ceiling for the symmetry lever, and we sit at ~93–95% of it.
